"The origin of the letters"

Where does the alphabet come from?


The origin of the alphabet is believed to have begun in ancient Egypt, when writing already had more than a millennium of existence. The first known alphabet emerged around 2000 BC, and it is derived from an alphabetical adaptation of Egyptian hieroglyphs. Today, almost all alphabets of the World descend directly from this first alphabet, or were inspired by its design.


The first purely alphabetic writing is believed to have developed around 1850 BC. C. by Semitic workers established in Sinai, giving the Egyptian glyphs phonetic values of their Semitic language. Over the next five centuries, this writing spread northward, and all later alphabets around the world descend from it, or have been inspired by one of its descendants.

What was the first writing?


The first writings were implemented by the inhabitants of this Mesopotamia, the Sumerians, approximately 3000 years BC. It was called cuneiform writing, because its characters were wedge-shaped.
